I've been trying to get to know the D3 visualization library for javascript. Here are some links that have come in handy.

  • D3.js Playground

    online coding/experimentation environment for D3 users. 3 input panels: one each for your d3 javascript code, css, and the data itself. A large output panel shows your results live as you alter your code

  • Scott Murray's D3 Tutorials

    These tutorials are the basis for an O'Reilly book. Lots of good step-by-step explanations for beginners.

  • D3 Homepage

    Too much cool stuff here. Nice documentation, a wide variety of eye-catching examples, links to source code...
